Percentage Calculator

Calculate percentages in three common ways: X% of Y, X is what percent of Y, and percentage increase or decrease from an old value to a new value.

Formula

Percent of = X/100 × Y
Percent share = X/Y × 100
Change = (New - Old)/Old × 100

Example

15% of 200 is 30. If 30 is compared to 200, then 30 is 15% of 200. If a value rises from 200 to 250, the increase is 25%.

How This Percentage Calculator Works

A percentage calculator is useful whenever a number needs to be understood as a part of one hundred. Percentages appear in marks, discounts, tax, salary hikes, interest rates, statistics, exam results and business reports. This page combines the three most common percentage tasks in one focused calculator so you do not need separate tools for each small calculation.

The first mode, X% of Y, answers questions like “What is 18% of 750?” It divides X by 100 and multiplies the result by Y. The second mode, X is what percent of Y, compares one value with another. It is useful for marks, completion rates and contribution percentages. The third mode calculates percentage change, which tells you how much a value increased or decreased compared with its original value.

When using percentage change, the old value should go in X and the new value should go in Y. A positive result means an increase, while a negative result means a decrease. The formula depends on the old value, so the old value cannot be zero. For real-life use, round percentages according to the context. Marks may need two decimals, while shopping discounts usually work fine as whole numbers.

FAQ

What is 10 percent of 500?

10% of 500 is 50 because 10/100 × 500 = 50.

How do I calculate percentage increase?

Subtract old from new, divide by old, then multiply by 100.

Can percentage change be negative?

Yes. A negative percentage means the value decreased.
